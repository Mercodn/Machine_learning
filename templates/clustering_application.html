K-Means Clustering Application

Interactive customer segmentation using K-Means algorithm (1000+ customers)

Problem Description

A retail company maintains a customer database with information about spending patterns and product preferences. The goal is to identify distinct customer segments for targeted marketing and personalized service strategies. By applying K-Means clustering, the company can automatically discover natural groupings in customer behavior.

How K-Means Clustering Solved This Problem

Benefits of K-Means for Customer Segmentation:

  • Automatic Discovery: Without manual categorization, K-Means identified 3 natural customer segments
  • Actionable Insights: Each cluster has distinct characteristics enabling targeted strategies
  • Scalability: Works efficiently even with 1,000+ customers
  • Interpretability: Results are easy to understand and explain to stakeholders
  • Continuous Improvement: Can be re-run periodically as customer base evolves

Key Takeaways

What We Learned

K-Means is powerful for unsupervised customer segmentation, enabling data-driven business decisions without pre-existing labels.

Next Steps
