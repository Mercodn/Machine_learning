📊 K-Means Manual Exercise - Invisible Maps Activity

Step-by-step simulation of the K-Means algorithm with 100 customer records

Exercise Overview

This manual exercise demonstrates how K-Means clustering works by performing 3 complete iterations on a dataset of 100 customer records with 2 features:

Feature 1: Monthly Spending (dollars)

Feature 2: Product Diversity (number of different products)

Number of Clusters: 3 (K=3)

Dataset Description

The dataset represents 100 customers with their monthly spending patterns and product diversity:

  • Monthly Spending Range: $500 - $9,500
  • Product Diversity Range: 2 - 50 different products
  • Data Points: 100 customer records

Initial Setup: Manually Selected Centroids

Before running K-Means, we manually selected 3 initial centroids:

Cluster Monthly Spending ($) Product Diversity
Centroid 1 $1,500 10
Centroid 2 $5,000 25
Centroid 3 $8,500 40

Euclidean Distance Formula

For each data point, we calculate distance to all 3 centroids using:

distance = √[(spending₂ - spending₁)² + (diversity₂ - diversity₁)²]

Example: For a customer with spending=$2,000 and diversity=15:

Distance to C1 = √[(2000-1500)² + (15-10)²] = √[250000 + 25] = √250025 ≈ 500.02
